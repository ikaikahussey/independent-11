The second Rell Sunn Aloha Jame will take place on November 28 at 6:00 p.m. The fun-filled event will take place in the North Shore Waimea Valley. Tickets range from $25 for general admission to $80 for a variety of VIP tables.

Local entertainment includes Henry Kapono, Amy Hanialii, the Young Coconuts, Kaukahi, Ten Feet, the Honolulu Lounge Lizards, and Te Lumanaki.

Local artists Wade Koniakowsky, Clark Little, Susan Wickstrand-Roche, Clark Takashima, Skye Walker, Heather Brown, and Julie Goldstein are just some of the artists who have donated artwork for the event. Proceeds from the sanctioned Vans Triple Crown event go to the Rell Sunn Educational Fund, a public benefit nonprofit corporation organized and operated exclusively for charitable, scientific, and educational purposes.

According to the organization’s website, the organization intends to educate the public about sportsmanship and the environment, combating juvenile delinquency, and providing monies to battle breast cancer.

For more information, visit www.rellsunn.com.

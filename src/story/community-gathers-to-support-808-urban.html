In case you missed it: there was a silent auction with local artwork, including pieces from Na Mea Hawai‘i; ‘ono food from Street Grindz; beats from Seph 1; the wrap of Dana Paresa’s Little Black Bag exhibit; macaroons from Cakeworks; and a healthy supply of sidewalk to chalk.

808 Urban is an important organization, channeling the experiences of local youth into powerful, public art. Learn more about them at 808urban.org.

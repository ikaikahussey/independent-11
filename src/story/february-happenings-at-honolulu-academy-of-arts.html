Exciting things are in store this February as the Academy presents an action-packed schedule. Get ready for our big Spring exhibition From Whistler to Warhol: Modernism on Paper, the return of ARTafterDARK after a three-month hiatus, Makaha Sons at the Doris Duke Theatre, a celebration of the Year of the Tiger at Bank of Hawaii Family Sunday, and more.

Hina Matsuri: Japanese Dolls from the Tsuji Family Collection
Feb. 11 - June 6, 2010
Gallery 20
Just in time for Girls Day, the Academy presents three sets of dolls from the Edo-Meiji (1868-1912) periods. The Tsuji family collected the dolls in the early 20th century and donated them to the museum in 2002. This exhibition is the Academy?s way of thanking the Tsuji family for its generous gift and of offering the community wishes for an auspicious spring season.

From Whistler to Warhol: Modernism on Paper
Feb. 18 - May 2, 2010
Henry R. Luce Gallery (28)
In an unprecedented move, the Academy will present a major exhibition of 100 masterworks from its modernist print collection, including a number of unpublished and rarely exhibited examples, as well as a selection of important recent acquisitions. Take a trip through art history, beginning with the American painter-printmaker James McNeill Whistler, and ending with 1960s and 70s? art stars such as Jasper Johns, Ellsworth Kelly, Roy Lichtenstein, Robert Rauschenberg, and Frank Stella. Like Hokusai?s Summit, this exhibition has fun interactive elements and engaging programming . Special members? reception is Feb. 17. If you?re not yet a member , it?s not too late to join.

Living Tradition: The Calligraphy of Chen Wei-Teh
Feb. 25 - June 20, 2010
Maurice J. Sullivan Gallery (16)
Considered the most personal, intimate, and revealing of all Chinese art forms, calligraphy remains a vital, living tradition in Chinese communities around the world. The Academy presents the first ever U.S. exhibition of the calligraphy of Chen Wei-Teh, one of Taiwan?s most renowned practitioners.

82nd Annual Honolulu Printmakers Exhibition
Feb. 25 - Mar. 19, 2010
Academy Art Center main gallery
See works by artists throughout the state, selected by juror Helen Frederick. Don’t miss the commissioned gift print by Maui printmaker Paul Mullowney. The print itself is a closely guarded secret until its unveiling at the opening reception on Feb. 24.

Artist-in-Residence: Elizabeth Curtis
Through Feb. 28
The Gallery 31 Art Studio Artist-in-Residence welcomes its third artist to the program, photographer Elizabeth R. Curtis. She is working on a project entitled The Visitors, and she needs people like you to be the subjects of her photographic portraits. See how you can be a part of the process.

Honolulu Chamber Music Series: Tokyo String Quartet
Feb. 3 at 6:30 p.m.
$50; $45 Academy members
Always wanted to hear what a Stradivarius sounds like? Don’t miss the internationally acclaimed Tokyo String Quartet. The four musicians?Martin Beaver, Kikuei Ikeda, Kazuhide Isumura and Clive Greensmith?perform on the four Stradivarius instruments known as the Paganini Quartet (on loan from the Nippon Music Foundation). The sound will be sublime. The virtuosos will play a program of Berg’s Quartet for Strings, Op. 3, Beethoven’s Quartet in E-flat Major, Op. 74, “Harp” and Bart?k’s Quartet No. 1, Op. 7. You don’t have to love classical music to appreciate this one.

CitySolve Urban Race Honolulu
Feb. 20, 11 a.m.-4 p.m.
The Honolulu Academy of the Arts partners with CitySolve Urban Race this month for its first-ever race in Hawaii and the kick-off of its 2010 series. Already spanning 25 cities nationwide, CitySolve tests the speed at which you and your partner can solve puzzles and race to locate stops across the city. It’s the road race for runners who like to think and thinkers who like to run. Families and seniors are welcome too. Winners get cool prizes and proceeds from the Honolulu race go to the Academy. Find out how to enter!

Bank of Hawaii Family Sunday: Year of the Tiger
Feb. 21, 11 a.m.-5 p.m.
Celebrate Chinese New Year at the Academy with art activities and entertainment related to the New Lunar Year.

M?kaha Sons Anniversary Celebration
Feb. 22 at 7 p.m.
All seats $30
Experience a night of Hawaiian music with the legendary M?kaha Sons.

ARTafterDARK: Mad for Modern
Feb. 26; 6-9 p.m.
After a three-month hiatus, Honolulu?s most popular monthly warm-up is back with ?Mad for Modern,? highlighting the exhibition From Whistler to Warhol: Modernism on Paper. Get moving to music by DJ Nicky Savage and Clones of the Queen. Enjoy food by Town and a special cocktail by Downtown.

The Academy Shop Annual Clearance Sale
Members? preview: Feb. 12
Open to public: Feb. 13, 14, 16
You have only three days (four if you?re a member) to take advantage of massive discounts (up to 80% off!). Stock up on stationery, art books, posters, jewelry, and ethnic clothing.

3rd Annual Bollywood Film Festival
Through Mar. 2, Doris Duke Theatre
See the best of recent Indian cinema. Mixed in with the dazzling musicals are independent and ?parallel cinema??more narrative, personal types of film.

Early Korean Ceramics: The Gaya Confederacy
Through Feb. 14, 2010
Gallery 14
See exquisite stoneware from 4th- to 6th-century Korea?specifically the enigmatic group of city-states along the southern coast of the Korean peninsula.

Tumultuous Traditions: Chinese Ink Painting in the 20th Century
Through Feb. 21, 2010
Maurice J. Sullivan Gallery of Chinese Art (16)
From the early innovators from China who suffered for their art during the Cultural Revolution, to present-day expatriates, this exhibition examines some of the many experiments in Chinese ink painting that developed over the course of the 20th century, as artists contended with the shifting currents of their craft and the social ramifications that went along with them.

In Honor of Grandmother
Through Apr. 4, 2010
The Textile Gallery (22)
From a feather lei to a Civil War-era quilt, this exhibition illustrates how textiles intimately connect people and integrate family traditions. If you sent in images of your grandmother, see her in the tile mosaic artwork in the gallery beginning Feb. 5.

Graphic Cabinet 5: Private and Public Devotion
in Medieval Europe
Through May 9, 2010
Gallery 9
Everything is illuminated in this show of selections from the Academy?s modest yet rich selection of medieval manuscripts and stained glass. Learn how these objects were used as part of everyday religious practice. Graphic Cabinet is an ongoing series of focused exhibitions showcasing highlights from the Academy?s vast and significant collection of work on paper.

A Year of Charles Bartlett: Bartlett in Java and Ceylon
Through June 6, 2010
John Dominis and Patches Damon Holt Gallery (29)
The Academy ends its year-long celebration of the life and work of Charles Bartlett with this special installation that focuses on the artist?s travels in Java and Ceylon (now Sri Lanka).

Poets and Printmakers: Literary Themes in Japanese Prints
Through June 6, 2010
Robert F. Lange Foundation Gallery (21)
Prepared by graduate students from the Department of East Asian Languages and Literatures at the University of Hawai`i-Manoa, this exhibition shows the intimate connection between woodblock prints and literature in Japan.

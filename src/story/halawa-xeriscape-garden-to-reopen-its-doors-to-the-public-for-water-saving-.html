HALAWA—The Board of Water Supply’s (BWS) Halawa Xeriscape Garden, located at 99-1268 Iwaena Street Halawa Valley’s Central Park industrial area, will soon reopen to the general public.

Beginning Saturday, November 6 the community may once again enjoy self-guided tours, free garden mulch and water conservation workshops for adults and keiki. The garden’s regular open hours are Saturdays from 9:00 a.m. to 3:00 p.m.

The Halawa Xeriscape Garden is a three-acre demonstration garden that displays a series of unthirsty, water-conserving plants in a visually attractive garden. BWS opened the garden to the public in September 1989 as a way to educate Oahu residents on ways to save water outdoor in lawns, gardens, and landscaped areas.

For more information about the Halawa Xeriscape Garden or workshops offered, visit www.boardofwatersupply.com or call 748-5041.

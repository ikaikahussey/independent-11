A jack o’ lantern contest will be part of the two-day Kauai Homegrown Music Festival this weekend.

KAUAI—The 9th Kauai Homegrown Music Festival ? replete with a Halloween Extravaganza Circus Dance Party ? is set for October 31 and November 1 at The Ranch in Kilauea.

The two-day music fest is being staged by Dove Presents and includes music by Magick Wand, Animal Dream, The Yes Men, Pacific Sound Machine, Burning Bush, DJ Surf and J Raz from 4:00 p.m. to midnight on Halloween. Two DJs will keep things rolling, and fire dancers will provide additional entertainment. Kids can navigate their way through the keiki maze, and contests will be held to choose the best Halloween costume and jack o? lantern.

The festivities continue on Sunday with a ?Day of the Dead? celebration featuring seven bands. Chris Berry from Panjea, Elijah and The Band Of Light, Matt Bolton, Anjela Rose, Jeff Caldwell, Paia Stream, Malama Pono Allstars, The Happy Enchalata Band, The Free Kings, UnKO Funki and fire dancers will be performing from noon to 8:00 p.m., and a barter fair is also planned.

Food and beverages will be sold on site, and attendees are also welcome to bring their own, as well as chairs, blankets and umbrellas, as the event will proceed, rain or shine. No dogs are allowed.

The Ranch is located at 4620 Kuawa Rd., just south of Kilauea, on the road that leads to Malama Kauai and the former Guava Kai plantation and visitor center.

Tickets are $20 per day or $30 for a two-day pass. Children ages 12 and under will be admitted free. Tickets may be purchased at Healthy Hut, Papayas, Kauai Music and Sound, Progressive Expressions and Scotty’s Music, or on-line at www.dovepresents.com. Single day tickets will be sold at the gate.

For more details call (808) 635-5556.

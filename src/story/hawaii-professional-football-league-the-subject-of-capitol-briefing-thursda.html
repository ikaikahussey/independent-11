The House Committee on Economic Revitalization & Business will convene on Thursday, March 24 to hold an informational briefing on the Hawaii Professional Football League. The purpose of the briefing is to gain a better understanding of the Hawaii Professional Football League (HPFL). Discussion will revolve around the HPFL’s plans, business models, and how they intend to develop and expand in Hawaii and what effects it will have upon the economy of the State. Discussions will also include HPFL’s community outreach plans the effect of player development into other nationally recognized leagues as well as possible marketing opportunities in sports tourism and economic development.


HPFL briefing
Thursday, March 24 at 10:30 a.m.
State Capitol Conference Room 312

HONOLULU—The Public Lands Development Corporation, a brand-new state corporation charged with developing public lands, will meet for the first time on Monday, August 29 at 2:30 p.m. in the Kalanimoku Building.

This inaugural meeting was originally planned for last week, but was hastily cancelled because it didn’t meet state open access laws, according to a report by Environment Hawaii.

What to look for in this meeting: item VII on the agenda, dealing with the “Public Land Optimization Plan,” and item VIII, a discussion of potential projects for development.
